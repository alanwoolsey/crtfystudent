# crtfy Student 12 Month Product Plan

Last updated: 2026-06-04

## Executive Direction

crtfy Student should become the best admissions operating layer in the market.

The product should not become a SIS, a campaign builder, a chatbot platform, an event platform, or a financial-aid processing system. Those categories create implementation drag and pull the product away from the admissions work that matters most. crtfy Student should win as the admissions operating system that tells teams:

- who needs work now
- what is blocking movement
- what evidence is trusted
- what decision can be defended
- who is likely to enroll
- what handoff is at risk

The strategic wedge is transcript-led intelligence. The broader market position is admissions execution.

## Market Thesis

Colleges and universities do not need another place to store student records. They need an operating system for the messy handoffs between inquiry, application completion, document processing, review, decision, yield, and enrollment handoff.

The market is fragmented across:

- Admissions CRMs such as Slate and Element451.
- General CRMs and marketing tools such as Salesforce and HubSpot.
- SIS platforms such as Banner, Colleague, PeopleSoft, Jenzabar, and Workday Student.
- Document imaging systems.
- Transcript services.
- Financial aid systems.
- Departmental spreadsheets.
- Manual email and call workflows.

crtfy Student should be able to coexist with those systems at larger institutions and replace the admissions CRM layer at smaller institutions that cannot afford or operate a separate Slate or Element451 instance.

## Product Positioning

The simple claim:

> crtfy Student gets applications completed faster, decisions made faster, and deposits protected better.

The sharper category claim:

> crtfy Student is the Admissions Operating System for modern enrollment teams.

The buyer-friendly shorthand:

> A higher-ed CRM and so much more.

Use this shorthand when speaking to smaller schools that need one affordable admissions system. For larger schools, sharpen the message around coexistence:

> The operating layer above your CRM and SIS that turns admissions data into completed work.

## Brand Narrative

crtfy Student should own the phrase `Admissions Operating System`.

That phrase is broader and stronger than CRM. A CRM mostly manages contacts, communication history, campaigns, and pipeline records. An admissions operating system manages the actual work of enrollment:

- inquiries and applicants
- student context
- checklist completion
- transcript and document processing
- duplicate cleanup
- trust and provenance
- transfer evidence
- review readiness
- decision packets
- counselor work
- admitted-student yield
- melt prevention
- cross-office handoff
- operational reporting

The market message should be:

> crtfy Student is the Admissions Operating System: a higher-ed CRM, transcript intelligence platform, decision workspace, and enrollment workflow engine in one.

That does not mean crtfy Student becomes everything. It means the product becomes the admissions team's daily system of action while integrating with marketing, SIS, financial aid, and communication tools where those systems are better suited.

## Product Principles

1. Own the daily workflow, not every source system.
2. Integrate for signals; build for decisions and action.
3. Make transcripts a conversion asset, not just a document requirement.
4. Keep every recommendation explainable.
5. Make trust and provenance first-class.
6. Route work to the right human or agent.
7. Respect institutional systems of record.
8. Build role-complete workflows only where crtfy Student can be best in class.

## What We Should Integrate

### Marketing And CRM

Use third-party systems for broad marketing automation. For smaller schools, crtfy Student should be able to serve as the admissions CRM of record for inquiry, applicant, decision, yield, and handoff operations. For larger schools, crtfy Student should integrate with the existing admissions CRM and act as the execution layer above it.

Priority integrations:

- HubSpot for smaller and mid-market schools that want modern marketing automation.
- Slate for higher-ed-native CRM environments.
- Element451 for enrollment marketing and engagement signals.
- Salesforce for institutions already standardized on Salesforce.

What we should own when replacing the admissions CRM:

- inquiry record
- prospect and applicant profile
- source and campaign attribution
- counselor owner
- territory or segment assignment
- application status
- checklist state
- interaction history
- decision status
- yield and melt work state

What we should consume when coexisting with a CRM:

- lead source
- inquiry date
- campaign/source attribution
- event attendance
- email/SMS engagement summary
- recent web or portal activity
- counselor owner
- application-start status
- application-submitted status

What we should send back when coexisting with a CRM:

- transcript received
- checklist status
- readiness status
- trust hold
- decision recommendation status
- admitted/yield risk
- melt risk
- next best action

What we should not build:

- visual email builder
- full campaign cadence builder
- marketing landing page CMS
- event registration engine
- mass texting platform

Recommended model:

- crtfy Student owns admissions operations and the applicant record.
- HubSpot owns marketing automation, campaigns, forms, landing pages, and nurture.
- The SIS owns official student, registration, billing, and academic records.

## Q1: Months 1-3 - Make Admissions Work Executable

### Goal

Turn the current product into the daily system of action for admissions operations.

### Strategic Bet

The fastest path to market credibility is not more dashboards. It is letting admissions employees finish real work in the product.

### Month 1: Checklist And Completion Engine

Build:

- checklist templates by population:
  - first-year
  - transfer
  - graduate
  - international
  - readmit
  - dual credit
- required vs optional checklist items
- item aging
- due dates
- received, needs review, waived, complete, and blocked states
- source document linkage
- confidence score for auto-completed items
- completeness rules by population, program, and term

Ship:

- checklist template admin
- student checklist detail
- readiness reason detail
- audit history for checklist changes

Win condition:

- staff can tell exactly why an applicant is incomplete and what action will make the file complete.

### Month 2: Document-To-Checklist Automation

- received-not-indexed queue
- document-to-student matching confidence
- document-to-checklist matching
- auto-complete high-confidence checklist items
- route low-confidence items to human review

- batch intake operations for transcript packages

Improve:

- action history readability

Win condition:

- transcript processing directly moves applicants forward without staff manually reconciling every file.

  - document requested

- basic SLA tracking by owner
- stale item resurfacing
- route-to-owner and route-to-team actions

Integrate:

- HubSpot activity sync for logged outreach where HubSpot is used.
- Slate or CRM activity export where institution requires it.

Win condition:

- counselors can work incomplete applications from crtfy Student without maintaining a side spreadsheet.

## Pricing Logic

Pricing should reflect operational value, not seat count alone.

Recommended structure:

- platform fee by institution size
- applicant volume tier
- premium connector fees for SIS/CRM integrations
- add-on modules for Decision Intelligence, Yield/Handoff, and Graduate Review
- implementation fee based on connector complexity

Avoid pricing only by user seat. Admissions teams are cross-functional, and seat pricing discourages the exact collaboration the product needs to create.
