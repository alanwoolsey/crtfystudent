# Admissions Counselor Day In The Life

## Purpose

This document describes a realistic day for an admissions counselor using `crtfy student`. It focuses on the counselor’s operational workflow: moving students forward, resolving blockers, reviewing context, and coordinating next actions across the application.

---

## 8:00 AM - Start In Today's Work

The admissions counselor signs in and lands in `Today's Work`.

This is the command center for the day. It gives a fast read on:

- students who are incomplete
- students ready for review
- blocked or exception cases
- tasks that are urgent, due today, or soon

The counselor uses this page to decide where attention goes first instead of hunting through separate records one by one.

Typical morning questions:

- Which students are closest to completion?
- Which files are blocked on documents?
- Which students need outreach today?
- Which cases are waiting on another team?

---

## 8:15 AM - Work The Incomplete Queue

The counselor moves into `Incomplete Applications` to focus on students who are missing requirements.

From here they can quickly see:

- completion percentage
- missing or blocking items
- readiness posture
- recommended next action

The counselor is usually prioritizing:

- students who are one item away from completion
- students stalled for too many days
- students with strong fit but missing one key document

This is where the counselor starts turning a backlog into movement.

---

## 8:45 AM - Open Student 360 For Context

When a student needs attention, the counselor opens the `Student 360` page.

This page acts as the working record for the student and gives the counselor:

- summary and contact details
- current stage and readiness
- advisor ownership
- checklist progress
- document history
- academic and trust context when permitted

Instead of treating the transcript as the main object, the app keeps the student at the center and shows supporting evidence around that student.

This helps the counselor answer practical questions fast:

- What is still missing?
- What happened most recently?
- Is this ready for a decision?
- Is there a trust or review block?

---

## 9:15 AM - Clear Checklist Items

Inside `Student 360`, the counselor often works directly in the checklist.

They review the next-best actions and mark items complete when appropriate. That may represent:

- confirming a required step was finished
- acknowledging a reviewed item
- moving the student from blocked toward decision-ready

This is one of the counselor’s most repeated actions during the day because checklist movement drives queue movement everywhere else.

The counselor is not just updating status. They are reducing friction so that the file can advance.

---

## 10:00 AM - Review Documents When A File Is Stuck

If a student is blocked on document quality or matching, the counselor goes to `Documents Queue`.

This queue surfaces:

- the reason a document is problematic
- suggested next action
- document and transcript status
- latest run state

The counselor may use this page to understand whether the issue is:

- a bad match
- a processing failure
- a trust-sensitive problem
- a file that should be rerun or replaced

For most counselors, this is not a deep technical workflow. It is an escalation and triage surface that helps them decide whether they can unblock the file themselves or need help from processing or trust staff.

---

## 10:30 AM - Reprocess Or Replace A Transcript

When a transcript issue is operational rather than policy-based, the counselor may act directly.

If the same uploaded file should be rerun:

- click `Reprocess`

If a corrected file is available:

- click `Replace file`
- choose the replacement transcript

The app then tracks:

- agent run state
- transcript persistence state
- backend outcome codes and messages

The counselor does not need to understand the backend mechanics in detail. What matters is that they can see whether the retry succeeded, failed, or still needs attention.

---

## 11:00 AM - Check Exception Details

For harder cases, the counselor opens the document exception details drawer.

This view consolidates:

- issue label
- suggested action
- latest failure message
- run-level result
- action-by-action processing detail

This is useful when a counselor needs to answer:

- Why did the document fail?
- Is this a student problem, file problem, or system problem?
- Should I retry this, ask for a new document, or hand it to another team?

This page helps counselors communicate clearly with students and internal teams because it replaces vague failure language with concrete backend explanations.

---

## 12:00 PM - Conduct Outreach Based On Student Readiness

Around midday, the counselor uses what they learned from queues and Student 360 to decide outreach priorities.

Typical counselor actions outside the app may include:

- emailing students who are one document away
- calling high-fit students who are stalled
- following up after a transcript correction
- coordinating with internal evaluators or trust staff

What the app contributes is prioritization and evidence. It tells the counselor who needs attention and why.

---

## 1:30 PM - Work Ready-For-Review And Decision Boundaries

In the afternoon, the counselor checks `Ready for Review` and `Decision Studio` related flows, depending on permissions.

At this stage, the counselor is looking for:

- files that are complete and ready
- students waiting on evaluator action
- students who can move from operational readiness to formal review

Even when the counselor does not release decisions directly, they play a major role in making sure the file is clean enough for the next person to act quickly.

Their contribution is upstream quality and speed.

---

## 2:15 PM - Watch For Trust Or Review Blocks

Some students look complete but still cannot move forward because of:

- trust flags
- document anomalies
- review-specific blockers

The counselor may see this in:

- `Exceptions Queue`
- `Trust Center`
- the student’s readiness state inside `Student 360`

The counselor’s job here is to avoid false progress. A file that looks complete but is blocked by trust should not be treated like a normal ready file.

This protects both the school and the student experience.

---

## 3:00 PM - Return To Student 360 For High-Priority Cases

Counselors usually revisit a smaller set of high-priority students later in the day.

These may be:

- students very close to a decision
- students with time-sensitive outreach
- students whose transcript was reprocessed earlier
- students whose blockers were resolved during the day

This is often where the counselor confirms that earlier work actually changed the student’s state:

- checklist improved
- readiness changed
- transcript issue cleared
- next best action advanced

This closes the loop between queue work and actual student movement.

---

## 4:00 PM - Check Yield-Oriented Students

If the counselor also works admitted populations, they may check:

- `Admitted / Yield`
- `Deposit / Melt`

At this point the questions change:

- Which admitted students need intervention?
- Who looks likely to deposit?
- Who shows signs of melting?

The counselor is no longer just moving students to completion. They are protecting enrollment outcomes.

---

## 4:45 PM - End Of Day Triage

Before signing off, the counselor usually wants to leave the system in a clean state.

That means:

- obvious checklist updates are applied
- urgent students have next actions
- unresolved document failures are understood
- files needing escalation are clearly identifiable
- no important student is stuck silently

The counselor does not need every file finished by the end of the day. They need every important file accounted for.

---

## Summary

For an admissions counselor, `crtfy student` is primarily a workflow and prioritization system.

The counselor’s day centers on:

- `Today's Work`
- `Incomplete Applications`
- `Student 360`
- `Documents Queue`
- `Exceptions`
- decision-readiness and yield follow-through when applicable

The product helps the counselor spend less time searching and more time moving students forward with context, evidence, and clear next actions.
