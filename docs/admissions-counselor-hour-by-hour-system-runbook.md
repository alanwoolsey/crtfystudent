# Admissions Counselor Hour-by-Hour System Runbook

## Purpose

This runbook describes a normal admissions counselor day in `crtfy student` with exact system actions. It assumes the counselor is responsible for moving students through:

`Inquiry -> Prospect -> Applicant -> Incomplete -> Complete -> Admitted -> Deposited/Committed -> Registered`

Use this as a training checklist for what to click, what to review, and what to enter throughout the day.

---

## 8:00 AM - Sign In And Open Today's Work

1. Navigate to the crtfy student app.
2. Sign in with your assigned username and password.
3. Confirm the left navigation is visible.
4. Click `Today's Work` in the left navigation.
5. Review the top summary cards:
   - `Needs attention`
   - `Close to completion`
   - `Ready for decision`
   - `Exceptions`
6. Review the `Counselor workbench` section.
7. Click the `Due follow-up` bucket.
8. Scan each student row for:
   - student name
   - program
   - reason to act
   - next follow-up
   - last contact
9. Start with students that have overdue follow-up or urgent priority.

Expected result: the counselor knows the first set of students who need action today.

---

## 8:15 AM - Work Overdue Follow-Ups

1. Stay on `Today's Work`.
2. In `Counselor workbench`, keep `Due follow-up` selected.
3. For the first student, click `Log contact`.
4. In the counselor action modal:
   - Set `Action` to `Log contact`.
   - In `Next action`, enter the next concrete step, for example:
     - `Send missing transcript reminder`
     - `Confirm application fee payment`
     - `Schedule advising handoff`
   - In `Next follow-up`, choose the next date and time.
   - In `Note`, enter a short activity summary, for example:
     - `Called student. Left voicemail about missing official transcript.`
5. Click `Save action`.
6. Repeat for each overdue student that can be handled quickly.
7. If a student needs deeper review, click the student name or `Open student` from the work item to open `Student 360`.

Expected result: overdue follow-ups are logged, next actions are set, and future follow-up dates are recorded.

---

## 8:45 AM - New Inquiry First Touch

1. Navigate to `Today's Work`.
2. In `Counselor workbench`, click the `Inquiry` bucket.
3. Open the first inquiry by clicking `Open student`.
4. On `Student 360`, confirm the `Overview` tab is selected.
5. Review:
   - email
   - phone
   - city
   - source
   - population
   - program
6. If the program is wrong or still generic:
   - Find the `Program` dropdown in the overview details.
   - Select the correct degree or program.
   - Wait for the `Program updated.` confirmation.
7. Click the `Outreach` tab.
8. In the outreach form:
   - Set `Template` to `New inquiry`.
   - Set `Channel` to `Email`, `Text`, `Call`, or `Other`.
   - Review the generated `Subject`.
   - Edit the `Draft` so it sounds personal.
   - Set `Next follow-up` to the next reasonable follow-up date.
9. Click `Log outreach`.
10. Click the `Timeline` tab and confirm the communication appears.

Expected result: the inquiry receives first-touch outreach, the selected program is accurate, and the next follow-up is scheduled.

---

## 9:30 AM - Work Incomplete Applications

1. Click `Incomplete Applications` in the left navigation.
2. Use the page filters or search box to focus on the highest-priority students.
3. Look for students who are:
   - one item away from completion
   - stalled
   - missing transcript
   - missing essay, fee, recommendation, or other requirement
4. Click `Open student` for the first student.
5. On `Student 360`, click the `Checklist` tab.
6. Review `Next best actions`.
7. If an item has been received and should be accepted:
   - Click the item action button, such as `Mark reviewed` or `Mark complete`.
   - Confirm the checklist updates.
8. If the student still needs outreach:
   - Click the `Outreach` tab.
   - Choose the right template:
     - `Missing transcript`
     - `Missing requirement`
     - `Application help`
   - Edit the `Draft`.
   - Set `Next follow-up`.
   - Click `Log outreach`.
9. If another office owns the blocker, click the `Handoff` tab.
10. In `Create handoff`:
    - Set `Target` to the receiving team, for example `Admissions Operations`, `Financial Aid`, or `Registrar / Transfer Specialist`.
    - Set `Priority`.
    - Set `Status` to `Open`.
    - Enter `Owner` if known, or leave it as the target team.
    - Set `Due`.
    - In `Blocker / request`, enter the exact request, for example:
      - `Please verify whether official transcript was received from North Valley CC.`
11. Click `Create handoff`.

Expected result: incomplete students either move closer to complete status, receive outreach, or are routed to the right owner.

---

## 10:30 AM - Review Documents And Transcript Evidence

1. Click `Documents Queue` in the left navigation.
2. Review document rows for:
   - unmatched student
   - low confidence
   - failed or pending processing
   - trust-sensitive issue
3. Open the document or related student when needed.
4. If working from `Student 360`, click the `Documents` tab.
5. In `Transcript lineage`, click the transcript row you need to inspect.
6. In the transcript modal:
   - Review extracted courses.
   - Review credits, grades, term, year, rigor, and confidence.
   - Review the transcript document preview when available.
7. If the issue is a checklist blocker:
   - Return to the `Checklist` tab.
   - Mark the item reviewed or complete when appropriate.
8. If the issue needs processing or trust review:
   - Click the `Handoff` tab.
   - Create a handoff to `Admissions Operations` or the appropriate review team.

Expected result: document blockers are resolved, escalated, or clearly assigned.

---

## 11:15 AM - Review Transfer Credit And Program Fit

1. Open a transfer or college transcript student in `Student 360`.
2. On `Overview`, confirm the selected `Program`.
3. Find the `Course equivalency` card.
4. Click `Refresh catalog map` if the mapping needs to be updated.
5. Review the table columns:
   - `Transcript course`
   - `Credits`
   - `Catalog course`
   - `Degree requirement`
   - `Match`
6. Look at the header totals:
   - `Might transfer`
   - `Likely won't`
7. If the map supports a stronger program conversation:
   - Click `Outreach`.
   - Choose a relevant template, usually `Application help` or `New inquiry`.
   - Add transfer credit context to the `Draft`, for example:
     - `Your transcript shows several courses that may apply toward the selected program.`
   - Click `Log outreach`.
8. If the mapping needs academic review:
   - Click `Handoff`.
   - Set `Target` to `Academic Department` or `Registrar / Transfer Specialist`.
   - In `Blocker / request`, enter the course mapping question.
   - Click `Create handoff`.

Expected result: transfer students get a clearer path to degree fit, and unresolved equivalency questions are assigned.

---

## 12:00 PM - Midday Queue Check

1. Return to `Today's Work`.
2. Review:
   - `Handoff queue`
   - `Post-admit blockers`
   - `Recruitment follow-up`
3. In `Counselor workbench`, click each relevant pipeline bucket:
   - `Applicant`
   - `Incomplete`
   - `Complete`
   - `Admitted`
   - `Deposited/Committed`
4. For each bucket, confirm whether the top students have:
   - current next action
   - owner
   - due date or next follow-up
   - recent activity
5. For any student with missing ownership:
   - Open the student.
   - Add a timeline note or create a handoff.

Expected result: the counselor catches stale work before the afternoon.

---

## 1:00 PM - Move Complete Files Toward Review

1. Click `Ready for Review` in the left navigation.
2. Review students whose files are complete.
3. Open a student that appears ready.
4. In `Student 360`, click `Decisions`.
5. Review:
   - recommendation summary
   - fit narrative
   - release posture
6. If the student should move to a reviewer or decision team:
   - Click `Handoff`.
   - Set `Target` to `Application Reviewer`.
   - Set `Priority` based on urgency.
   - Set a due date.
   - In `Blocker / request`, enter:
     - `Application complete and ready for review. Please review decision packet.`
   - Click `Create handoff`.
7. Return to `Today's Work` and continue with the `Complete` bucket.

Expected result: complete files are not stuck with the counselor. They are routed to review ownership.

---

## 2:00 PM - Work Admitted Students

1. Click `Admitted / Yield` in the left navigation.
2. Choose a view such as:
   - `Newly admitted`
   - `High likelihood`
   - `No recent activity`
   - `Missing next step`
3. Open a student.
4. In `Student 360`, click `Yield / Deposit`.
5. Review:
   - deposit likelihood
   - fit score
   - owner
   - post-admit readiness percentage
6. If the student needs a deposit reminder:
   - Click `Outreach`.
   - Set `Template` to `Deposit reminder`.
   - Choose `Channel`.
   - Edit the `Draft`.
   - Set `Next follow-up`.
   - Click `Log outreach`.
7. If affordability or aid is the blocker:
   - Click `Handoff`.
   - Set `Target` to `Financial Aid`.
   - In `Blocker / request`, enter the aid question or package concern.
   - Click `Create handoff`.

Expected result: admitted students have next-step outreach or a clear downstream owner.

---

## 3:00 PM - Work Deposited / Committed Students

1. Click `Deposit / Melt` in the left navigation.
2. Choose a view such as:
   - `At risk`
   - `Missing FAFSA`
   - `Missing orientation`
   - `Registration incomplete`
3. Open a student.
4. In `Student 360`, click `Yield / Deposit`.
5. In `Post-admit readiness`, update each relevant milestone:
   - Set `Status`.
   - Confirm or edit `Owner`.
   - Set `Due`.
   - Enter `Blocker` if there is one.
   - Click `Save` for that milestone.
6. Common milestone updates:
   - `Financial aid package`: set to `Blocked` if package is missing.
   - `Orientation`: set to `In progress` or `Complete`.
   - `Advising appointment`: enter owner and due date.
   - `Registration status`: set to `Blocked` if registration is incomplete.
   - `Bursar / student account`: set to `Blocked` if there is an account hold.
7. If another office must act:
   - Click `Handoff`.
   - Choose the correct `Target`.
   - Enter blocker details.
   - Click `Create handoff`.

Expected result: deposited students have visible registration and enrollment blockers with owners and due dates.

---

## 4:00 PM - Log Recruitment And Source Activity

1. Open the relevant student in `Student 360`.
2. Click the `Recruitment` tab.
3. In `Recruitment source`:
   - Select `Territory`.
   - Select `Event type`, for example:
     - `College fair`
     - `High school visit`
     - `Transfer partner visit`
     - `Open house`
     - `Webinar`
     - `Campus visit`
   - Set `Date`.
   - Enter `School / partner`.
   - Enter `Event name`.
   - Enter `Notes`, for example:
     - `Met student at transfer fair. Interested in BS Computer Science and asked about transfer credits.`
4. Click `Log recruitment activity`.
5. Click `Timeline`.
6. Filter or scan for `recruitment_event` and confirm the event appears.

Expected result: inquiries and applicants are tied to territories, schools, partners, and events for follow-up and reporting.

---

## 4:30 PM - Check Reporting And Accountability

1. Click `Reporting` in the left navigation.
2. Review the `Overview` cards:
   - incomplete to complete conversion
   - average days to complete
   - average days complete to decision
   - admit to deposit conversion
   - melt rate
3. Review `Inquiry to registered funnel`.
4. Review `Handoff SLA`:
   - open handoffs
   - overdue handoffs
   - completed percentage
5. Review `Counselor workload`.
6. Review `Source performance`.
7. If a metric looks off:
   - Return to `Today's Work`.
   - Open the relevant bucket.
   - Create or update student actions, handoffs, or milestones.

Expected result: the counselor sees whether the day is improving movement through the funnel and whether ownership gaps remain.

---

## 4:45 PM - End-Of-Day Cleanup

1. Return to `Today's Work`.
2. Click `Due follow-up`.
3. Confirm every student touched today has:
   - next action
   - next follow-up date
   - last contact or activity note
4. Review `Handoff queue`.
5. For any handoff created today, confirm it has:
   - target team
   - owner or queue
   - due date
   - priority
   - blocker/request
6. Review `Post-admit blockers`.
7. Confirm blocked milestones have owners and due dates.
8. Open any student with missing context and add a `Timeline` note:
   - Click `Timeline`.
   - Set `Activity` to `Note`.
   - Set `Outcome` to the closest available value.
   - Enter the note.
   - Click `Add activity`.

Expected result: no touched student is left without the next clear step.

---

## 5:00 PM - Sign Out

1. Click the user menu in the top-right corner.
2. Click `Sign out`.
3. Confirm the login screen appears.

Expected result: the counselor ends the day with follow-ups, handoffs, outreach, milestones, and activity history recorded in the system.

---

## Daily Quality Checklist

Before leaving, confirm:

- [ ] Every contacted student has a timeline entry.
- [ ] Every student needing future action has `Next follow-up` set.
- [ ] Every blocked student has either outreach or a handoff.
- [ ] Every handoff has target team, owner, due date, priority, and blocker/request.
- [ ] Every admitted or deposited student has post-admit milestones updated.
- [ ] Every recruitment conversation has source, territory, school or partner, and event context logged.
- [ ] Today's Work no longer has avoidable stale items in `Due follow-up`.
